LAWRENCE

Welcome to Lawrence

Lawrence, Kansas, the home of Kansas University, is located in the northeast region of the state between the Kansas and Wakarusa Rivers. It has a rich civil war history; the city was named after an abolitionist from Massachusetts, Amos A. Lawrence. This city has seen small growth due to the constant flow of college kids coming in and out of the town

  • City Population: 94,934
  • Incorporated: 1858
  • Region: Northeast Kansas
  • Classification: Urban
  • Median household Income: $55,598

Lawrence, Kansas was named after Amos A. Lawrence, a Republican abolitionist from Massachusetts who provided financial aid and support for the community.

Massachusetts Street, often referred to as “Mass Street” has become the central street of Lawrence due to its founders roots in Massachusetts. It has a collection of many dining, shopping, and entertainment options.
